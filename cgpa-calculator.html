How to Calculate CGPA

CGPA is calculated by dividing the total grade points by the number of subjects.

CGPA Formula:
Total Grade Points / Number of Subjects

This CGPA calculator helps students quickly calculate their academic performance.

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It represents the average of grade points obtained in all subjects during a semester or across multiple semesters.

CGPA is commonly used by universities and colleges to measure a student’s overall academic performance.

What is SGPA?

SGPA stands for Semester Grade Point Average. It represents the grade point average of a student in a single semester.

Multiple SGPAs together are used to calculate the final CGPA.

Terms Used in CGPA Formula

  • Total Grade Points: The sum of grade points obtained in all subjects.
  • Number of Subjects: The total number of subjects considered for calculation.
  • Grade Point: A numerical value assigned to a grade (for example, A = 10, B = 8, etc.).

Difference Between CGPA and SGPA

SGPA is calculated for one semester, while CGPA is calculated by averaging SGPAs of all semesters completed.
